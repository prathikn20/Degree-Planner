===============================================================================
SYSTEM CONSTRAINTS ARCHITECTURE FOR DEGREE PLANNER OPTIMIZATION ENGINE
===============================================================================

1. INTRA-PROGRAM EXCLUSIVITY CONSTRAINT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: A single unique canonical course identity cannot occupy more than 
  one Demand Slot within the exact same degree program track.
* Container Course Exception: Independent studies, undergraduate research, or 
  rotating special topics seminars (e.g., numbers ending in 93, 95, 96, 99 or 
  matching "is_repeatable": true) are exempt from this binary block. Their 
  exclusivity is governed by a threshold capacity counter: 
  Count(Canonical_ID) <= Max_Allowed_Enrollments.

2. TRACK-CONTEXTUALIZED PREREQUISITE SATISFACTION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: No course can be scheduled unless at least one valid prerequisite 
  pathway defined in the catalog is completely satisfied by either the student's 
  completed credits or other planned courses sequenced in strictly prior terms.
* Cross-Listing Handling: Prerequisite chains are stored dynamically as a 
  dictionary mapped to specific track IDs to prevent cross-track prerequisite 
  bypasses. The engine enforces the strict prerequisite pathway mandated by the 
  slot's parent Track_ID for major requirements, while allowing a flexible 
  bracketed "OR" structure for neutral General Education slots.

3. FOCUS CAPACITY (FC) SINGULARITY CONSTRAINT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: While any course (including core major requirements, IDST, or 
  FAD options) can satisfy a General Education Focus Capacity (FC) slot via its 
  attributes, a single unique canonical course node cannot be assigned to more 
  than one FC slot simultaneously across the graduation plan.
* Mathematical Expression: Sum_over_fc_slots(Indicator(Course C assigned)) <= 1

4. THE 50% PROGRAM EXCLUSIVITY RULE (IDENTITY PROTECTION)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: For any degree program track P (Major or Minor), the number of 
  core slots filled by courses unique to P (not shared with any other concurrent 
  track in the active plan) must be strictly greater than half of the total 
  core slots demanded by that program.
* Mathematical Expression: Exclusive_Core_Slots(P) > (Total_Core_Slots(P) / 2)
* Lockout Mitigation: Mandatory, single-candidate required core course slots 
  (domain size = 1) where the student has zero elective flexibility are excluded 
  from the Total_Core_Slots denominator. This prevents small-scale minors from 
  triggering unresolvable domain collapses due to structural major overlaps.

5. FIRST-YEAR FOUNDATION XOR CONSTRAINT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: A student's career graduation plan can contain at most one 
  introductory foundational first-year course. Enrollment in an FY-SEMINAR and 
  an FY-LAUNCH are strictly mutually exclusive.
* Mathematical Expression: Planned(FY-SEMINAR) + Planned(FY-LAUNCH) <= 1
* Execution: The moment one type is logged as completed or active, the alternative 
  attribute is permanently purged from all remaining unassigned slot domains.

6. SLOT SUFFICIENCY & REAL-CREDIT ACCUMULATION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: Every generated Demand Slot must be fully and mathematically 
  satisfied by meeting its exact course-count target or minimum credit hour threshold.
* Variable-Credit Handling: Credit-sufficed elective slots must evaluate the 
  strict summation of actual assigned credit hours. Variable-credit courses 
  default to their minimum value during the search unless explicitly overridden 
  by historical user transcript data to prevent credit miscalculations.

7. LONGEST-PATH TEMPORAL CEILING CONSTRAINT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Description: Prevents the engine from recommending deep, narrow linear 
  prerequisite chains that are physically impossible to complete within the 
  student's remaining time horizon.
* Handling: The engine calculates the longest linear prerequisite chain depth 
  (max_depth) of the proposed schedule. If max_depth > Remaining_Semesters, 
  a massive credit penalty is applied, forcing the solver to select a wider, 
  parallelizable layout of alternative courses.
