NOTE: Taught in English, credit given for PORT major if some or all work is done in Portuguese. What and how much work is done in Portuguese to be determined by a discussion with the instructor based on student’s current level of Portuguese.
NOTE: This is a one-credit hour, once-a-week course. Students may repeat for credit since this is a rotating topics course.
NOTE: With the approval of the undergraduate advisor for Portuguese, students majoring in Romance languages with an emphasis in Portuguese may count up to nine hours in related areas in such departments, programs, and curricula as history, art, music, cultural studies, and women’s and gender studies.
NOTE: Because few entering students have completed coursework in Portuguese prior to enrolling in the University, it is essential that interested students start the language as early as the first year. Completion of PORT 204 or PORT 402 or equivalent is a prerequisite for the major courses taught in Portuguese. Students may begin taking PORT courses taught in English prior to fulfilling this requirement.
