NOTE: The minor consists of five courses, which must be taken in at least two different departments; at least one course must be from the Department of Religious Studies, and at least one course must be at an advanced level (400 and above).
### Core Requirements
  RELI 180 | Introduction to Islamic Civilization 1 | 3hrs
  OR or HIST 138 | History of Muslim Societies to 1500
RULE: One of the following three courses: 3
  HIST 139 | History of Muslim Societies since 1500 | hrs
  HIST 276 | The Modern Middle East | hrs
  RELI 181 | Modern Muslim Societies | hrs
RULE: Three elective courses (see course lists below) 9
### Additional Requirements
RULE: Three of the five courses in the minor (including the required courses listed above) must be selected from one of two subject categories—either Islamic studies or Middle Eastern studies—as indicated by the two lists of approved courses below.
RULE: The other two courses must come from the other category.
NOTE: Both courses cover material from the seventh century to 1500
NOTE: One language course at level 5 or higher in Arabic, Hebrew, Hindi-Urdu, Persian, or Turkish can count as a course toward the minor. Up to two courses in Islamic and Middle East studies at Duke University may count toward the minor if approved by the director of the minor.
NOTE: The minor is housed in the Department of Religious Studies and directed by Dr. Juliane Hammer, a member of the department, who is responsible for advising all students who minor in Islamic and Middle Eastern studies (as necessary beyond general academic advising).
