## Pathways

## Actuarial Science
NOTE: This pathway equips students to quantify and manage financial risk in uncertain, high-stakes settings like insurance, pensions, healthcare, investments, and enterprise risk management. Students learn to model future outcomes using probability, statistics, financial mathematics, and modern data analytics—turning messy real-world information into clear assumptions, defensible forecasts, and actionable decisions. The concentration combines rigorous mathematical foundations with professional communication, regulatory and ethical judgment, and practical computing tools to prepare students for actuarial internships and the credentialing exam pathway. Graduates are prepared to explain complex risk tradeoffs plainly, build models that hold up under scrutiny, and support organizations in making long-term promises they can actually keep.
NOTE: Students must take 3 of the following courses:
  STOR 470 | Fundamentals of Actuarial Mathematics | 3hrs
  STOR 471 | Long-Term Actuarial Models | 3hrs
  STOR 472 | Short Term Actuarial Models | 3hrs
  STOR 473 | Financial Mathematics for Actuarial Science | 3hrs

## Decision Analytics
NOTE: This pathway is for students who are interested in focusing their studies on making data-driven decisions in complex systems. While statistical techniques are very helpful for making decisions in data-rich settings, stylized formulations can be more helpful for generating insights and making decisions in the absence of sufficient data. This concentration brings together both statistics and operations research-based courses together to offer students a path for getting a more holistic training in analytical decision making.
NOTE: Students must take 3 of the following courses:
  STOR 475 | Healthcare Risk Analytics | 3hrs
  STOR 512 | Optimization for Machine Learning and Neural Networks | 3hrs
  STOR 543 | Dynamic Decision Analytics | 3hrs
  STOR 545 | Stochastic Models and their Applications | 3hrs
  STOR 572 | Simulation for Analytics | 3hrs

## Machine Learning
NOTE: This pathway combines advanced statistical training with enhanced computational and data analytic training for those planning careers in information intensive industries or research. Students will extend their ability to model and analyze data, using mathematical and computational methods to make predictions and decisions in the face of uncertainty. The concentration is focused on fundamental training in mathematics and applied statistics, including specialized courses with an emphasis on statistical computing, and machine learning.
NOTE: Students must take 3 of the following courses:
  STOR 512 | Optimization for Machine Learning and Neural Networks | 3hrs
  STOR 556 | Time Series Data Analysis | 3hrs
  STOR 557 | Advanced Methods of Data Analysis | 3hrs
  STOR 565 | Machine Learning | 3hrs
  STOR 566 | Introduction to Deep Learning | 3hrs
